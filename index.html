The basics of a good prompt

Every strong prompt has a few ingredients. Think of it like giving clear instructions to a helpful colleague.

Be specific

Instead of "write something about dogs," try "write a 100-word paragraph about why golden retrievers make good family pets."

Set format & length

Ask for bullets, paragraphs, steps, or a table. Say how long: "in 3 sentences," "in under 200 words," or "as a list of 5 items."

Define the audience

"Explain like I'm 10" or "for a technical audience" helps the AI match tone and complexity to who will read it.

Add constraints

Constraints focus the answer: "without jargon," "using only US data," or "avoid mentioning X" keeps output on track.

Powerful techniques

Level up with these proven prompt patterns.

01

Role-playing

Start with a role: "You are a patient high-school physics teacher." The AI will adopt that persona and style.

02

Few-shot examples

Show one or two examples of the output you want. "Format your answers like this: [example]. Now do the same for..."

03

Chain of thought

Ask the AI to "think step by step" or "show your reasoning." Great for logic, math, and complex decisions.

04

Negative instructions

Say what to avoid: "Don't use bullet points," "Don't mention competitors," or "Keep it under 50 words."

Pro tips

1

Iterate: your first prompt rarely needs to be perfect. Refine based on the output.

2

Break big tasks into smaller prompts (outline first, then expand each part).

3

Paste key context (documents, data) when relevant so the AI has something to work with.

4

Ask for one thing at a time when possible; multi-part questions can get mixed results.
